# You Don’t Need to Know Everything—You Need to Know What Matters

Let’s be honest: if you’re a CTO these days, it can feel like you’re standing in the middle of a tech bazaar, with every stallholder shouting about the latest framework, tool, or AI model. Every week, something new drops. Every conversation feels like a pop quiz you didn’t study for. And beneath it all, there’s that quiet, persistent fear: if you’re not on top of every trend, every shift, you’re falling behind.

I’ve been there. I’ve felt that pressure—the gnawing sense that you’re only as good as your knowledge of the latest shiny thing. But let’s call this what it is: FOMO, not leadership. And it’s exhausting.

## The Myth of the All-Knowing CTO

Somewhere along the way, we started believing that technical leadership meant omniscience. That to be credible, you had to have an opinion on every new JavaScript framework, every cloud service, every AI breakthrough. I’ve seen CTOs run themselves ragged trying to keep up, only to end up overwhelmed and, ironically, less effective.

Here’s the truth: you don’t need to know everything. You need to know what matters.

## Clarity Over Comprehensiveness

When I work with CTOs, the first thing I help them do is make peace with not knowing it all. That’s not a cop-out—it’s a strategic advantage. The real skill isn’t encyclopaedic knowledge; it’s the ability to filter out the noise and zero in on what will actually move the needle for your organisation.

Let me give you a concrete example. I once worked with a CTO who was convinced they needed to overhaul their entire stack because “everyone” was moving to microservices. The team was already stretched thin, and the business wasn’t suffering from monolith-related issues. After mapping out their systems of work and identifying their real constraints, it became clear that the bottleneck was actually in their deployment pipeline, not their architecture. Chasing the microservices trend would have been a costly distraction.

## Mapping What Matters

So, how do you get clear on what matters? Here’s what’s worked for me and the leaders I’ve coached:

- **Map your systems of work.** Where does value flow? Where does it get stuck? Don’t just look at the tech—look at the people, the processes, the handoffs.
- **Uncover your strategic constraints.** What’s actually holding you back? Is it technical debt, lack of automation, unclear priorities, or something else entirely?
- **Identify the technical capabilities that drive outcomes.** Not every capability is equally important. Some things can wait; others are critical. Learn to tell the difference.

This isn’t about ignoring trends. It’s about being intentional. Not every new thing deserves your attention right now. Some things are noise; some are signals. The trick is knowing which is which.

## Leadership Is Focus, Not FOMO

The best leaders I know aren’t the ones who can recite the latest Gartner hype cycle from memory. They’re the ones who can say, “That’s interesting, but it’s not a priority for us right now.” They lead with clarity and intent, not by reacting to every headline.

I’ll be blunt: if you’re spending your days chasing every new trend, you’re not leading—you’re following. And your team can feel it. They need you to filter, to focus, to help them see what matters.

## Practical Steps to Cut Through the Noise

If you’re feeling overwhelmed, here’s what I recommend:

1. **Audit your attention.** Where are you spending your time and energy? Is it on things that actually drive outcomes, or just on what’s new and shiny?
2. **Map your value streams.** Get a clear picture of how work flows through your organisation. Where are the real bottlenecks?
3. **Prioritise ruthlessly.** Not everything is urgent. Learn to say no—or at least, not now.
4. **Build a trusted network.** You don’t have to know everything yourself. Surround yourself with people who can help you spot what matters.
5. **Reflect regularly.** Take time to step back and ask: are we focusing on what will actually make a difference?

## Challenge: What Will You Stop Reacting To?

So, here’s my challenge to you: What’s one thing you’re currently reacting to—one trend, one tool, one “must-have” feature—that you could safely ignore for now? What would it look like to lead with clarity and intent, rather than FOMO?

You don’t need to know everything. You just need to know what matters—and have the courage to focus on it.

Let’s stop pretending otherwise. Your team, your business, and your sanity will thank you.
